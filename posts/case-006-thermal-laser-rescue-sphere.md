## How the Idea Came to Life
The core architecture of this system originated years ago while planning a snowboarding trip with my brother. We were discussing severe environments: how do you signal your exact physical position if you get completely separated or buried under zero visibility? 

The initial concept was a passive locator, but when high-visibility atmospheric green lasers and high-energy thermal lasers entered the market, the blueprint evolved into an active search and rescue concept.

## How It Can Help
Traditional avalanche rescue operations take too much time because emergency teams have to search the snow layer by layer. This system could help save critical time. 

The skier or mountaineer carries a compact sphere. Inside, a gyroscope automatically forces the laser to point straight up at the sky. Then, the thermal laser melts a clean vertical hole through the snow and shoots a solid green light beam high into the air, creating an instant visual beacon for rescue teams.
